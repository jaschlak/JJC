# JJC V1 Parts List

Goal: design a simple printable juggling club for Jordan's Juggle Clubs around a dowel rod spine. This version does not include LEDs, electronics, or diffuser parts.

## Printer Constraints

- Printer: Creality Ender 3 V3 SE
- Build volume: 220 x 220 x 250 mm
- Planned material: mostly TPU
- Design assumption: the full club will be printed in sections

## Parts

### 1. Dowel Rod

- Purpose: main rigid spine along the club axis
- Runs through the handle and body
- Printed parts should slide over or mount around it
- Exact diameter should be measured before finalizing hole sizes

### 2. Dowel Centering Ring

- Priority: first part to design and print
- Purpose: keeps the dowel centered inside the hollow club body
- Shape: washer-like ring
- Good first test because it is small, fast, and checks dowel fit

### 3. Handle Sleeve

- Purpose: grip surface around the dowel
- Likely material: TPU
- Can be one sleeve or several shorter grip sections

### 4. Knob Cap

- Purpose: rounded end cap at the handle end
- Likely material: TPU
- Should protect the dowel end and take drops

### 5. Body-to-Handle Collar

- Purpose: transition from the narrow handle to the wider club body
- Likely material: TPU
- Helps define the club shape and supports the lower body shell

### 6. Lower Body Shell

- Purpose: lower half of the hollow club body
- Likely material: TPU
- Designed around the dowel and centering rings

### 7. Upper Body Shell

- Purpose: upper half of the hollow club body
- Likely material: TPU
- Connects to the lower body shell and nose cap

### 8. Nose Cap

- Purpose: rounded top cap of the club
- Likely material: TPU
- Should be replaceable if it gets damaged

### 9. Section Couplers

- Purpose: align and connect printed shell sections
- Could be slip-fit rings, threaded sleeves, or simple keyed joints
- Material can be TPU or a stiffer filament depending on the fit needed

## Suggested Build Order

1. Buy and measure the dowel rod
2. Dowel centering ring
3. Handle sleeve test
4. Knob cap
5. Short hollow body shell test
6. Body-to-handle collar
7. Upper/lower body shell sections
8. Nose cap
9. Section couplers

## First Print Recommendation

Start with the dowel centering ring. It will test:

- Fusion scripting workflow
- dowel fit
- printer dimensional accuracy
- TPU flexibility
- how much clearance the dowel needs
